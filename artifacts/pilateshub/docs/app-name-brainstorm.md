## Direction 1: Pilates-Inspired Wordplay

Playing on Pilates terminology: reformer, flow, core, alignment, center, control, contrology, precision, breath, powerhouse, spring, carriage, hundred, teaser, scoop, C-curve, imprint.

### 1.1 — **Pilotée**

- **Tagline:** "Your Pilates, piloted."
- **Why it works:** A brilliant French-English double entendre. "Pilotée" (French, feminine form of "piloted/guided") sounds almost identical to "Pilates" spoken quickly. It implies guidance, direction, and being in control of your journey — all core Pilates principles. The feminine form subtly nods to the primary audience.
- **Potential issues:** Pronunciation may confuse English-only speakers. "Pilote" apps exist in French (project management tools), but none in the fitness space. The accent mark may cause technical issues in some contexts.

### 1.2 — **The Hundred**

- **Tagline:** "Your first hundred is just the beginning."
- **Why it works:** "The Hundred" is the iconic opening exercise in classical Pilates — every practitioner knows it. It also suggests community scale (hundreds of studios, hundreds of members), gamification (hit your hundred), and ambition. Clean, memorable, story-rich.
- **Potential issues:** Generic in search results. "The Hundred" is a common phrase. May need strong branding to own the term. No direct app conflicts found in fitness.

### 1.3 — **Powerhaus**

- **Tagline:** "Activate your powerhouse."
- **Why it works:** "Powerhouse" is the Pilates term for your core — the center of all movement. Spelling it "Powerhaus" (German nod, like Joseph Pilates' heritage) makes it distinctive, modern, and trademarkable. Suggests strength, energy, community hub.
- **Potential issues:** Multiple "Powerhouse Pilates" studios and apps already exist. The German spelling helps differentiate but may confuse some users.

### 1.4 — **Springboard**

- **Tagline:** "Spring into your best self."
- **Why it works:** Springs are the resistance mechanism in every Pilates reformer — they're literally what makes Pilates work. "Springboard" also means a launching platform, perfectly capturing the app as a launchpad for your Pilates journey. Double meaning is elegant.
- **Potential issues:** "Springboard" is a common English word used by many apps and companies across industries (education, tech, finance). Would be difficult to own in search/SEO.

### 1.5 — **Teaser**

- **Tagline:** "The ultimate Pilates challenge awaits."
- **Why it works:** The Teaser is one of the most aspirational advanced Pilates exercises — a benchmark move that practitioners work toward. It perfectly captures the gamification/challenge aspect of the app. Playful, intriguing, and deeply Pilates.
- **Potential issues:** "Teaser" has other connotations (movie teasers, strip-tease) that could dilute the brand. May feel exclusionary to beginners.

### 1.6 — **Contrology**

- **Tagline:** "The original science of control."
- **Why it works:** Joseph Pilates originally called his method "Contrology." It's the most authentic, historically rooted name possible. Signals expertise, heritage, and seriousness about the craft.
- **Potential issues:** **Already taken.** Balanced Body owns the Contrology brand for classical Pilates equipment and has a website (contrology.pilates.com). There is also a "Contrology Pilates" app and a "Contrology Pilates Club" app on both iOS and Android. This name is essentially unavailable.

### 1.7 — **Imprint**

- **Tagline:** "Leave your imprint."
- **Why it works:** "Imprint" is a foundational Pilates position (imprinting the spine into the mat). It also means to make a lasting mark — perfect for a social platform where you track progress and leave your impression on the community.
- **Potential issues:** Fairly generic word. Used in publishing ("imprint" of a publisher). Would need strong visual branding to stand out. No major fitness app conflicts found.

## Direction 2: French/Parisian Elegance

French words or Franco-English blends that feel chic, premium, and globally aspirational.

### 2.1 — **Alignée**

- **Tagline:** "Beautifully aligned."
- **Why it works:** French for "aligned" (feminine form). Alignment is a core Pilates principle. The word feels elegant, precise, and aspirational. The accent gives it Parisian flair. Works in both French and English contexts.
- **Potential issues:** Multiple "Align" fitness apps already exist (Align Pilates and Wellness, Align Fitness, AlignUs, Aligned Fitness). The French spelling with the accent differentiates it, but there's crowding in the "align" namespace. Spelling may be challenging for non-French speakers.

### 2.2 — **Bien Formée**

- **Tagline:** "In perfect form."
- **Why it works:** French for "well-formed" or "in good shape." Directly ties to the "Good Form" kudos feature. Sounds luxurious and Parisian. Captures both physical form (body) and good form (technique/social praise). A "Pilates Bonne Forme" studio app exists but "Bien Formée" is distinct.
- **Potential issues:** Three syllables in French, could feel long for an app name. May be confused with existing "Forme" apps (FORM by Sami Clarke, FORME Personal Training, La Forme — all exist).

### 2.3 — **L'Atelier**

- **Tagline:** "Where movement is crafted."
- **Why it works:** French for "the workshop" or "the studio." Connotes artisanal craft, care, and Parisian creative culture. Positions Pilates as an art form, not just exercise. Premium and sophisticated. Evokes the idea of a curated space.
- **Potential issues:** Very common in French business naming (restaurants, boutiques, hotels). "L'Atelier Fitness" exists as a gym in France. Not specific enough to Pilates. Apostrophe in the name can cause technical issues.

### 2.4 — **Maison Pilates**

- **Tagline:** "Welcome home to your practice."
- **Why it works:** "Maison" (house/home) gives it a luxury-brand feel (Maison Margiela, Maison Kitsuné). Positions the app as the definitive home of Pilates. Warm yet prestigious. Clear category identification.
- **Potential issues:** Long for an app name (13 characters). Very literal. Could feel pretentious. Harder to abbreviate casually.

### 2.5 — **Tenue**

- **Tagline:** "Hold your center."
- **Why it works:** French for "held/sustained" (as in holding a position) and also "outfit/attire." Beautiful double meaning for an app that combines Pilates practice with a fashion/apparel marketplace. Chic, short, memorable.
- **Potential issues:** No fitness app by this name found — good availability. May be confused with "tenue" meaning "outfit" only, missing the fitness connection for French speakers. English speakers won't know the word.

### 2.6 — **Grâce**

- **Tagline:** "Move with grace."
- **Why it works:** French for "grace" — both elegance of movement and gratitude. Captures the aesthetic of Pilates beautifully. Short, universally understood concept. The accent mark adds French distinction.
- **Potential issues:** Very common word. Multiple apps/brands use "Grace" in fitness/wellness. The accent helps differentiate, but SEO would be challenging.

### 2.7 — **Belle Ligne**

- **Tagline:** "Your beautiful line."
- **Why it works:** "Ligne" in French means "line" (as in body line, the long lines created in Pilates) and also "figure" (as in "garder la ligne" = keep your figure). "Belle Ligne" evokes elegant body lines, the aesthetic of Pilates alignment, and the visual beauty of good form.
- **Potential issues:** Two words. Sounds like it could be a fashion brand or cosmetics line. Not immediately suggestive of a fitness app or community.

## Direction 3: Movement & Body

Words evoking movement, body awareness, fluidity, and physical intelligence.

### 3.1 — **Cadence**

- **Tagline:** "Find your rhythm."
- **Why it works:** Cadence means rhythmic flow of movement — perfect for Pilates, which emphasizes flowing transitions. Also implies pace, consistency, and the rhythm of a regular practice (ties to streaks/habits). Musical and elegant.
- **Potential issues:** "Cadence Physio & Pilates" already has an app on both iOS and Android. "Cadence Performance" and "Cadence Retreats" also exist. The name space is occupied.

### 3.2 — **Kinesia**

- **Tagline:** "The art of intelligent movement."
- **Why it works:** From Greek "kinesis" (movement). Scientific yet beautiful. Suggests deep body intelligence and intentional movement — exactly what Pilates is about. Distinctive, easy to pronounce globally, and not obviously taken in the fitness app space.
- **Potential issues:** Could sound clinical or medical. "Kineo" apps exist (Kineo Fitness & Wellness, Kineo by Technogym). "Kinesia" would need to differentiate from these.

### 3.3 — **Ondulée**

- **Tagline:** "Ride the wave of movement."
- **Why it works:** French for "undulating/wave-like." Captures the fluid, wave-like quality of Pilates movement. Sensual, elegant, distinctly French. The double 'e' ending is visually pleasing. Truly unique — not found as any existing app.
- **Potential issues:** Difficult to spell for non-French speakers. Pronunciation uncertainty ("on-doo-LAY"). May sound too abstract for a fitness app.

### 3.4 — **Glide**

- **Tagline:** "Glide into your best self."
- **Why it works:** The carriage on a Pilates reformer literally glides. It also describes the smooth, effortless quality that Pilates aims for in movement. Short, active, premium-sounding. Easy to spell, say, and remember globally.
- **Potential issues:** Common English word. Various apps use "Glide" (Glide is a major app-building platform). Would need strong sub-branding.

### 3.5 — **Supple**

- **Tagline:** "Strength meets flexibility."
- **Why it works:** Captures the core promise of Pilates — becoming strong AND flexible. The word feels luxurious and sensual. Short, distinctive in the fitness app space.
- **Potential issues:** Could sound slightly suggestive to some audiences. Not commonly associated with fitness apps. May not translate well into French/other languages.

### 3.6 — **Articulate**

- **Tagline:** "Every movement, expressed."
- **Why it works:** In Pilates, articulation (of the spine, of joints) is a key concept. "Articulate" also means to express clearly — tying to the social/community aspect of the app. Intelligent, distinctive.
- **Potential issues:** Long word (4 syllables). Sounds more intellectual/academic than fitness. Not immediately intuitive as a fitness app name.

### 3.7 — **Lengthen**

- **Tagline:** "Lengthen. Strengthen. Connect."
- **Why it works:** One of the most-used cues in Pilates ("lengthen your spine," "lengthen through the crown of your head"). Captures the elongating, decompressing quality of Pilates. Active verb, clear, aspirational.
- **Potential issues:** Not exciting as a brand name. Sounds like a single feature rather than a platform. Seven letters is manageable but not snappy.

## Direction 4: Community & Social

Names that suggest connection, togetherness, tribe, and the social fabric of Pilates culture.

### 4.1 — **Le Cercle**

- **Tagline:** "Your Pilates circle."
- **Why it works:** Directly references the "Pilates Circle" (Magic Circle) — one of the most iconic Pilates props. "Cercle" is French for "circle," evoking community, belonging, and exclusivity (inner circle). Ties perfectly to the "Pilates Circles" forums feature. Parisian, social, premium.
- **Potential issues:** "Cercle" is used by various fitness brands (Le Cercle Boxing in Paris is well-known). "Circle" fitness apps exist (Circle Fit, Circolo). The boxing gym association in Paris could cause confusion.

### 4.2 — **The Studio**

- **Tagline:** "Every studio. One community."
- **Why it works:** Simple, clean, and directly communicates the studio-discovery core feature. "Studio" implies craft, artistry, and intentional practice. Positions the app as THE definitive studio platform.
- **Potential issues:** Extremely generic. Impossible to own in search. Many businesses and apps use "The Studio."

### 4.3 — **Ensemble**

- **Tagline:** "Stronger together."
- **Why it works:** French for "together." Universally understood thanks to music/fashion. Directly captures the social/community DNA of the app. Elegant, warm, inclusive.
- **Potential issues:** Common French word used by many brands. "Ensemble" apps exist in various categories. Hard to own in search.

### 4.4 — **Guild**

- **Tagline:** "Join the Pilates guild."
- **Why it works:** A guild is a community of craftspeople — positioning Pilates practitioners as artisans of movement. Suggests expertise, mentorship, belonging, and skill progression (matches the gamification/progression features). Short, strong, distinctive.
- **Potential issues:** Medieval/gaming connotations might clash with the modern, feminine, wellness aesthetic. Could feel exclusionary or too "serious."

### 4.5 — **Collectif**

- **Tagline:** "Move as one."
- **Why it works:** French for "collective." Suggests community, shared purpose, and creative collaboration. Feels premium and Parisian. Captures the social aspect without being generic.
- **Potential issues:** Used by various fashion and creative brands. "Collectif" is a vintage fashion brand. Could feel impersonal (collective vs. individual journey).

### 4.6 — **Convive**

- **Tagline:** "Come alive, together."
- **Why it works:** From "convivial" — warm, friendly, social. Also suggests "come + live/vive" (live/alive in French/Spanish). Perfect blend of social warmth and vitality. Distinctive, short, and cheerful.
- **Potential issues:** Not immediately associated with fitness. Could be mistaken for a dining/social app. In French, "convive" means a dinner guest — which could be charming or confusing.

### 4.7 — **Lien**

- **Tagline:** "The link between you and your practice."
- **Why it works:** French for "link/bond/connection." Short, elegant, captures the social-connection DNA. Also works phonetically in English. Four letters — extremely app-friendly.
- **Potential issues:** In English, "lien" is a legal term (a claim on property). This could create negative associations for English-speaking users. Pronounced "lee-EN" in French but "LEEN" in English.

## Direction 5: Short & Punchy (App-Friendly)

1–2 syllable names that are easy to type, remember, and say across languages.

### 5.1 — **Plié**

- **Tagline:** "Bend. Don't break."
- **Why it works:** A fundamental movement in both ballet and Pilates. One syllable (in practice), instantly evocative of graceful, controlled movement. French origin makes it chic. The accent gives it visual distinction. No "Plié" fitness app found in searches.
- **Potential issues:** More associated with ballet than Pilates specifically. Means "bent" — could the name feel like it's about bending/folding rather than strength? The accent may be dropped by users ("Plie").

### 5.2 — **Cōr**

- **Tagline:** "From the core."
- **Why it works:** Derived from "core" — the absolute center of Pilates practice. The macron (ō) adds visual distinction and premium feel. Three letters. Punchy, strong, memorable. Also evokes Latin "cor" (heart).
- **Potential issues:** "Core" fitness apps abound (Core Home Fitness, Core: Fitness & Nutrition by Chloe Ting, Cora AI Fitness Coach, Kore Fitness App). Extremely crowded namespace. The macron may cause typing friction.

### 5.3 — **Hāl**

- **Tagline:** "Breathe. Move. Live."
- **Why it works:** Derived from "exhale/inhale" — breath is a foundational pillar of Pilates. Extremely short (3 letters). The macron adds elegance. Feels minimal and premium, like a luxury brand.
- **Potential issues:** Too abstract. No inherent meaning for most users. Could be confused with "HAL" (the AI from 2001: A Space Odyssey) or various other uses of the word.

### 5.4 — **Sway**

- **Tagline:** "Move your way."
- **Why it works:** Evokes gentle, controlled movement. Friendly, approachable, slightly playful. Four letters. Easy to say in any language. Suggests influence ("hold sway") and rhythmic motion.
- **Potential issues:** Doesn't feel premium enough for the target positioning. More "casual lifestyle" than "sophisticated Pilates." Various apps use "Sway."

### 5.5 — **Āxis**

- **Tagline:** "Find your center."
- **Why it works:** The axis is the central line around which the body rotates — fundamental to Pilates alignment. Scientific, strong, gender-neutral. Works as both a Pilates term and a metaphor for the app being the central axis of your fitness life.
- **Potential issues:** Multiple fitness businesses use "Axis." Could feel cold or too geometric.

### 5.6 — **Poised**

- **Tagline:** "Perfectly poised."
- **Why it works:** Means balanced, composed, ready — everything Pilates cultivates. Also suggests elegance and grace. Six letters, two syllables, easy to spell. Premium connotation.
- **Potential issues:** Static-sounding. Doesn't suggest movement or energy. Could feel too passive for a social/gamified app.

### 5.7 — **Nōya**

- **Tagline:** "Your new center."
- **Why it works:** An invented word that sounds like "noyau" (French for "core/nucleus"). Short, feminine, globally pronounceable. The macron adds distinctiveness. Feels fresh, modern, premium.
- **Potential issues:** No inherent meaning — requires brand-building from scratch. Could be mispronounced. The connection to "noyau" is subtle and may be lost on most users.

## Direction 6: Invented/Portmanteau

Made-up words combining concepts from Pilates, French culture, community, and wellness.

### 6.1 — **Pilaté**

- **Tagline:** "Pilates, elevated."
- **Why it works:** "Pilates" + the French past participle accent, creating a word that looks like a refined, French-kissed version of Pilates. The accent transforms the familiar word into something new, premium, and ownable. Reads as "pee-lah-TAY" — musical and elegant. No app by this name found.
- **Potential issues:** Very close to "Pilates" — could be seen as generic or descriptive rather than a brand name. Trademark challenges since it's essentially a stylized version of a common word.

### 6.2 — **Reformée**

- **Tagline:** "Reformed. Refined. Renewed."
- **Why it works:** Blends "Reformer" (the iconic Pilates machine) with the French feminine suffix "-ée." Suggests transformation, renewal, and the reformer apparatus. Elegant and distinctive. Captures the progression/journey aspect of the app.
- **Potential issues:** "Reformée" in French literally means "reformed" (as in discharged from military service or a reformed institution) — potentially awkward connotation. Also evokes religious reformation.

### 6.3 — **Flōtis**

- **Tagline:** "Flow through Pilates."
- **Why it works:** "Flow" + "Pilates" compressed into a single coined word. Musical, easy to say, distinctly ownable. The macron on the 'o' adds premium visual flair. "Flow" captures the movement quality; "-tis" retains the Pilates DNA.
- **Potential issues:** Sounds vaguely medical (like "arthritis," "tendonitis"). This suffix association could undermine the premium, aspirational positioning.

### 6.4 — **Corēa**

- **Tagline:** "Move from your core."
- **Why it works:** "Core" + a suffix that sounds like the French/Latin feminine ending. Also evokes "choreography" and "Korea" (a country known for wellness culture). Musical and distinctive. No exact match found as a fitness app.
- **Potential issues:** Could be confused with the country "Korea." "Corea" is also a surname (Chick Corea). The connection to "core" might not be immediately obvious.

### 6.5 — **Pivolve**

- **Tagline:** "Pivot. Evolve."
- **Why it works:** "Pivot" (a turning point, rotation) + "evolve" (growth, transformation). Already sounds like it could be a real word. Captures both the physical movement of Pilates and the personal evolution journey. Dynamic and forward-looking.
- **Potential issues:** **Already exists.** P.volve (pvolve.com) is an established fitness method and app. Too close for comfort — likely trademark conflict.

### 6.6 — **Soūlis**

- **Tagline:** "Where soul meets movement."
- **Why it works:** "Soul" + "Pilates" suffix. Captures the mind-body-spirit dimension of Pilates that goes beyond mere exercise. Feminine, ethereal, distinctive. The macron adds visual intrigue.
- **Potential issues:** Could sound too spiritual/New Age for a social/gamified app. Might attract the wrong audience expectations. "Soul" is used extensively in fitness (SoulCycle).

### 6.7 — **Centrō**

- **Tagline:** "Your center for everything Pilates."
- **Why it works:** Based on "center/centre" — the fundamental Pilates principle (centering is one of the six principles). The accent makes it feel Italian/Mediterranean and premium. Also literally means the app is your "center" or hub. "Centr" exists (Chris Hemsworth's app) but "Centrō" is distinct.
- **Potential issues:** Close to "Centr" (existing major fitness app). Could seem like a copycat or cause confusion. The accent may be dropped.

## Direction 7: Emoji-Friendly / Visual

Names that pair well with an icon, emoji, or visual identity.

### 7.1 — **Spring**

- **Tagline:** "Where Pilates springs to life."
- **Icon:** A coiled spring, or a spring (season) blossom
- **Why it works:** Pilates reformers use springs for resistance — it's the mechanical heart of the apparatus. "Spring" also means the season of renewal, and "to spring" means to leap forward. Triple meaning: equipment, rebirth, energy. Pairs beautifully with a coiled-spring icon or a blossom.
- **Potential issues:** Extremely common word. Spring Health (mental health app) is well-known. Many apps use "Spring."

### 7.2 — **Ring** (or **Le Ring**)

- **Tagline:** "Step into the ring."
- **Icon:** The Pilates Magic Circle / Ring
- **Why it works:** The Pilates Ring (Magic Circle) is one of the most visually iconic and recognizable Pilates props. It's literally a circle — perfect for an app icon. "Ring" also evokes community (ring of people), notification (your phone rings), and arena (step into the ring). "Le Ring" adds French flair.
- **Potential issues:** "Ring" is Amazon's doorbell brand. Extremely common word. "Le Ring" might sound like a boxing reference.

### 7.3 — **Blossom**

- **Tagline:** "Watch your practice blossom."
- **Icon:** A flower opening / unfolding
- **Why it works:** Captures growth, beauty, and the unfolding journey of a Pilates practice. Feminine, warm, aspirational. The visual of a flower blooming pairs perfectly with progression tracking and leveling up. Works well with seasonal campaigns.
- **Potential issues:** Very feminine — could limit male audience appeal. "Blossom" is used by various wellness apps. Not specific to Pilates at all.

### 7.4 — **Spark**

- **Tagline:** "Ignite your practice."
- **Icon:** A small spark or flame
- **Why it works:** Energy, initiation, inspiration. Suggests the moment of motivation to book a class or start a challenge. Short, energetic, positive. Works well with gamification (earning sparks, spark streaks).
- **Potential issues:** Common word used by many tech companies (Spark email, Apache Spark, etc.). Not specific to Pilates or movement.

### 7.5 — **Coil**

- **Tagline:** "Uncoil your potential."
- **Icon:** A spiral/coil (like a reformer spring)
- **Why it works:** Springs coil and uncoil — a perfect metaphor for the tension-and-release of Pilates. Also suggests stored energy, potential, and the spiral motions common in Pilates exercises. A coil/spiral makes a striking, minimal app icon. Unique in the fitness space.
- **Potential issues:** Could sound industrial or mechanical. Doesn't immediately evoke wellness or community. "Coil" has contraceptive associations (IUD coil) in some markets, especially the UK.

### 7.6 — **Aura**

- **Tagline:** "Radiate from within."
- **Icon:** Glowing rings / radiant circle
- **Why it works:** Suggests the radiance and energy that comes from a strong Pilates practice. Connects to the mind-body-wellness dimension. Beautiful, feminine, aspirational. A glowing-circle icon is visually stunning and app-store-friendly.
- **Potential issues:** "Aura" is a well-known meditation/sleep app with millions of users. Direct competitor in the wellness app space. Name is effectively taken.

### 7.7 — **Bloom**

- **Tagline:** "Bloom where you're planted."
- **Icon:** A flower in bloom, or a human figure opening up
- **Why it works:** Growth, beauty, opening, flourishing. Pairs naturally with progression features (blooming through levels). Feminine without being exclusionary. Short, warm, positive. The visual possibilities are rich — petals could represent different aspects of the app (studio, social, shop, stats).
- **Potential issues:** "Bloom" is used by several fitness/wellness apps and brands. Not specific to Pilates.

## TOP 5 Overall Picks

### #1: Pilotée

**The case:** This is the name that makes you stop and think — and then smile when you get it. "Pilotée" is a masterclass in naming: it sounds almost identical to "Pilates," it's French (Parisian launch), it means "guided/piloted" (the app guides your journey), and the feminine form subtly speaks to the primary audience. It works as an app name, a brand, a verb ("Get pilotée"), and a philosophy. The piloting metaphor extends beautifully: you pilot your practice, the app pilots your discovery, the community pilots each other. It's ownable, trademarkable, and no fitness app uses it. The only friction — pronunciation for pure English speakers — becomes a feature, not a bug: it invites curiosity and conversation.

**Suggested tagline:** "Your Pilates, piloted."
**App icon concept:** A stylized compass rose or flight path merged with a Pilates body silhouette.

---

### #2: Plié

**The case:** Sometimes the best names are hiding in plain sight. "Plié" is one syllable, universally associated with graceful physical movement, French by origin, and available in the fitness app space. It's the rare name that is both immediately understandable and chic. The accent mark gives it visual distinction on the app store. It works across the entire product: you "plié" into a class, your social feed, a challenge. It's small enough to be an icon (a human figure in a plié could be the logo). For a Parisian-born app targeting wellness-conscious women, this is pure brand gold. The ballet association is a feature — Pilates and ballet share DNA, audience, and aesthetics.

**Suggested tagline:** "Bend. Don't break."
**App icon concept:** A minimal figure in plié position, legs forming a diamond shape, in a single elegant line.

---

### #3: The Hundred

**The case:** Insider knowledge as brand identity. Every Pilates practitioner in the world knows "The Hundred" — it's the exercise you do in every single class, the one that makes you feel like you're truly doing Pilates. It's a built-in story, a built-in challenge (gamification: "Join The Hundred club"), and a built-in scale metaphor (hundreds of studios, hundreds of connections). It's English, globally pronounceable, and has no trademark conflicts in the fitness app space. It creates instant community — if someone says "I'm on The Hundred," other Pilates people immediately understand. The number "100" also makes for a bold, distinctive app icon.

**Suggested tagline:** "Your first hundred is just the beginning."
**App icon concept:** A bold, stylized "100" with flowing lines, or the letter "H" formed by a body in Pilates position.

---

### #4: Tenue

**The case:** The hidden gem. "Tenue" (French: "held/sustained" and "outfit/attire") is a word that does double duty for this specific app better than any other candidate. Pilates is about holding positions with control — tenue. The app has a marketplace for apparel and equipment — tenue. It's short (5 letters), elegant, French, and virtually uncontested in the app store. It won't mean anything to most English speakers on day one, but that's an advantage: it becomes a brand word, a lifestyle word, like "lululemon" once was. For a premium-but-accessible Parisian brand expanding globally, this mysterious-yet-beautiful quality is perfect.

**Suggested tagline:** "Hold your center."
**App icon concept:** A minimal, elongated figure in a sustained hold position, suggesting both strength and elegance.

---

### #5: Reformée

**The case:** The strongest portmanteau on the list. "Reformée" takes the most iconic piece of Pilates equipment (the Reformer) and transforms it into a French feminine adjective meaning "reformed/transformed." It tells the whole brand story in one word: this app will reform your practice, your body, your community, your relationship with Pilates. The "-ée" suffix is unmistakably French and feminine. It's fully invented as a brand name (no app conflicts found), highly trademarkable, and immediately evocative for anyone who knows what a Pilates reformer is. The transformation narrative ties directly into the progression/journey features of the app.

**Suggested tagline:** "Reformed. Refined. Renewed."
**App icon concept:** A reformer spring elegantly curving into the letter "R," or a silhouette transforming/emerging.

## Honorable Mentions

- **Ondulée** — Gorgeous and truly unique, but pronunciation is a barrier for global scale.
- **Imprint** — Smart Pilates reference with great metaphorical depth, but may feel too quiet as a brand.
- **Nōya** — Modern and distinctive invented word, but requires heavy brand-building with no inherent meaning.
- **Kinesia** — Scientific elegance, but risks feeling clinical rather than social and fun.
- **Convive** — Warm and social, but the distance from fitness/Pilates is too far.

## Naming Decision Framework

When selecting the final name, weigh these factors:

| Factor | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Distinctiveness** | High | Can you own it? Trademark, domain, app store, social handles |
| **Pilates resonance** | High | Does a Pilates practitioner immediately "get it"? |
| **French/global appeal** | High | Works in Paris launch AND global expansion |
| **Pronounceability** | Medium | Can people say it without instruction? |
| **Brevity** | Medium | Shorter is better for app names, hashtags, word-of-mouth |
| **Emotional warmth** | Medium | Does it feel welcoming, not cold or clinical? |
| **Versatility** | Medium | Can it extend to features (e.g., "Pilotée Circles," "The Hundred Challenge")? |
| **Marketplace fit** | Low-Med | Does it accommodate the shop/apparel dimension? |
