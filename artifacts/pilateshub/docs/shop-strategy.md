# PilatesHub Shop Strategy

> Last updated: 2026-03-23

---

## 1. Vision

The PilatesHub shop is a curated boutique — not an Amazon clone. Think **"Le Bon Marche meets Pilates."** Every product is handpicked, the presentation is editorial, and buying feels like treating yourself to a studio-quality experience at home.

**Why buy from us instead of Amazon?**

- **Curated by coaches and the community** — trust over algorithm
- **Pilates-specific** — no generic fitness junk
- **Social proof** — see what friends and coaches actually use
- **Exclusive PilatesHub branded products** — you cannot get these anywhere else
- **Studios can sell their own merch** — one platform, one audience

## 2. Business Models (Hybrid Approach)

We run three models simultaneously to balance margin, inventory risk, and breadth.

### A. PilatesHub Private Label (highest margin: 50-70%)

White-label products with our branding via print-on-demand (Printful) and wholesale suppliers.

**Research-backed pricing:**

- Grip socks wholesale: ~$1.50-4.00/pair depending on volume, customization, and grip type (PVC dot grip is cheapest; custom logo grip runs higher). Studios retail at $12-25/pair, meaning 65-80% margin is achievable at scale. For a 3-pack, wholesale cost lands around €4-6. Sources: [Tucketts Wholesale](https://tucketts.com/pages/wholesale), [Dead Soxy Bulk Guide](https://deadsoxy.com/blogs/custom-socks/custom-grip-socks-wholesale-bulk-guide), [Revel Athletic Wholesale](https://www.revelathletic.com/pages/wholesale-grip-socks)
- Printful apparel base costs (no subscription, no minimums): t-shirts ~$10-13, tank tops ~$9-13, hoodies ~$17-22, leggings ~$21-26, joggers ~$22-23. Sources: [Printful Custom Sportswear](https://www.printful.com/custom/collections/sportswear), [Printful T-shirt Pricing Calculator](https://www.printful.com/blog/how-to-price-custom-shirts)

- Minimum order quantities from wholesale grip sock suppliers start as low as 15 pairs (mix of styles), making initial testing low-risk

- This creates lock-in: studios need us for their merch channel

## 4. Merchandising & Curation

### Shop Layout

The shop page is designed editorially, not like a marketplace grid. Each section tells a story.

1. **Hero banner** — 1 featured product or collection, rotates weekly. Full-bleed imagery, editorial tone. Example: "Spring Reformer Edit: 5 pieces for your mat-to-brunch transition."
2. **"Coach Picks"** — Each partnered coach curates 5 favorites. Rotates monthly. Shows coach photo + quote. Builds trust and gives coaches a revenue channel.
3. **"Trending Now"** — Most wishlisted and purchased items this week. Auto-populated by data. Social proof engine.
4. **"New Arrivals"** — 3-5 new products added weekly. Keeps the shop feeling fresh and gives users a reason to come back.
5. **Category grid** — Browse by category with clean icons. 7 categories max on the main page.
6. **"The Essentials Kit"** — Beginner starter bundle (grip socks + resistance band + mat towel). One-click purchase. Always visible.
7. **"Seasonal Edit"** — Quarterly themed collection. Example: "Winter Warm-Up" (hoodie + grip socks + candle + collagen).

### Rotation Schedule

| Section | Frequency | Owner |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Hero banner | Weekly | Marketing/Content |
| Coach picks | Monthly | Coaches + Partnerships |
| Trending | Auto (by data) | Algorithm |
| New arrivals | Weekly (3-5 products) | Product/Ops |
| Seasonal edit | Quarterly | Marketing + Coaches |
| Essentials kit | Evergreen (update quarterly) | Product |

## 5. Social Commerce

This is our differentiator. The shop is not a standalone page — it is woven into the social fabric of the app.

### "What I Wore"
- Users tag their outfit/gear in session posts
- Tagged products become shoppable links
- Creates organic UGC-driven product discovery
- Example: "Sophie just completed a Reformer Flow wearing Grip Socks in Blush and the Reformer Addict tee" — both items are tappable

### Coach Storefronts
- Each coach gets their own shop page with curated picks
- Coaches earn affiliate commission on sales from their page (10-15%)
- Adapts the existing BrandPage component
- Coaches are incentivized to promote their storefront to their clients

### Wish Lists (Social)
- Users can create and share wish lists
- Visible to friends: "Sophie wants the Manduka PRO for her birthday"
- Enables gifting between community members
- Creates demand signal data for us (most wishlisted = we should stock it)

### Group Buys
- "5 people want grip socks this week — unlock 15% group discount"
- Creates urgency and social virality
- Works especially well for studio groups who train together
- Threshold-based: discount unlocks when N people commit

### Post-Session Prompt
- After completing a session, show a personalized product suggestion
- "Great Reformer session! Your grip socks are 3 months old — time for a fresh pair?"
- Contextual: suggest mat spray after mat class, grip socks after Reformer class
- Subtle, not aggressive — one small card, easy to dismiss

## 7. Studio Merch Program

### How It Works

1. **Studios apply** to sell through PilatesHub (verified studio accounts only)
2. **We provide Printful integration** — no inventory needed for anyone
3. **Studio uploads designs** (logo, slogans, artwork) — we create the product listings
4. **Revenue split: 70% studio / 30% PilatesHub**
5. **Verified studios** get a "Studio Shop" badge on their profile page
6. **Fulfillment** handled entirely by Printful (print, pack, ship)

### Available Products for Studios

| Product | Base Cost (Printful) | Studio Sets Price | Studio Gets (70%) | PilatesHub Gets (30%) |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Branded tee | ~€10 | €30 | €14 | €6 |
| Branded hoodie | ~€20 | €55 | €24.50 | €10.50 |
| Branded tank | ~€10 | €32 | €15.40 | €6.60 |
| Branded tote bag | ~€8 | €22 | €9.80 | €4.20 |

### Why Studios Want This

- No upfront cost (POD = zero inventory risk)
- Built-in audience (their students + broader PilatesHub community)
- Professional product pages (they do not need to build an e-commerce site)
- Merch builds studio brand loyalty

### Why This Is Strategic for PilatesHub

- Creates platform lock-in (studios depend on us for merch revenue)
- Every studio merch sale reinforces PilatesHub as the ecosystem
- Studios actively promote PilatesHub to sell their merch
- 30% platform fee is pure margin (Printful handles everything)

## 11. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Low conversion rate (<1%) | Medium | High | Start with hero product (grip socks) that has proven demand; use social proof heavily |
| Printful quality issues | Low | Medium | Order samples first; have backup POD provider (Printify, Gooten) |
| Wholesale inventory sitting unsold | Medium | Medium | Start with small MOQs (100-200 units); test demand with POD first before going wholesale |
| Studios not adopting merch program | Medium | Low | Offer first 10 studios free setup; show revenue projections |
| Affiliate programs changing terms | Low | Low | Diversify across 5+ brands; build private label as primary revenue |
